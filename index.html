The Physics Behind the Simulation

In a stable circular orbit, Earth's velocity is precisely balanced with the Sun's gravitational pull. The orbital velocity for a circular orbit is given by:

v = √(GM/r)

When we suddenly remove mass from the Sun, Earth's velocity remains unchanged but the gravitational pull decreases. The critical threshold is at 50% solar mass—at this point, Earth's existing velocity exactly equals the new escape velocity, resulting in a parabolic trajectory.

E = v²/2 − GM/r = 0 (at 50% mass)

Below 50%: Earth escapes on a hyperbolic path. Above 50%: Earth remains bound in an elliptical orbit, but with a much larger aphelion (farthest point from the Sun).

Light delay: In reality, gravitational changes propagate at the speed of light. Earth wouldn't "feel" the Sun's mass change for ~8.3 minutes. This simulation includes that delay.
